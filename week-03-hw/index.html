Rose Gold

by Victoria Lassettre

The golden sun fades in the winter,

But the pale silver of the moon shines

Piercing the dark, it falls on the snow pines

Lights dance on a snowflake; a frost glitter

Creating an iridescent splinter

So cold, the whispering wind sits and whines

But then teardrops will come to melt, the signs

That the spring will come forward and enter

Soon the earth will be speckled with gems

Roses and strawberries dot a garden bed

Blossoms bloom from their thick emerald stems

Colors enter the world, but none quite like red

Suns and stars are needed, and without them

This will never happen, or so they've said.
