Austria

Austria's Current Politcal System

A member of the European Union since 1995, Austria is currently a parliamentary republic with a Federal President who's term last for 6 years. Additionally, there are two chambers that make up their Parliament: the National Council and the Federal Council. The next general election for Austria is set for 2018. The country is divided into nine provinces with the capital in Vienna.

The Last Dynasty of Austria: the Habsburgs

Founded by Kaiser Rudolf I, the Habsburg dynasty ruled for 640 years until the death of Kaiser Franz Joseph I in 1916, and then the First Republic of Austria was established in 1918. The Habsburg rose to power thanks to their tactical marriage arrangements and their alliance with Poland, Venic, and Russia in the Turkish Wars. During their reign there was a marked change in society as the Zeitgeist changed and urbanization and industrialization became a central part of life. The final emperor, Kaiser Franz Joseph and his wife, Elisabeth "Sisi"--until her assasination in 1898--forged a strong front for Vienna as an epicenter for culture and metropolitan life. They also forged an alliance with Hungary creating the Austro-Hungarian Empire.
